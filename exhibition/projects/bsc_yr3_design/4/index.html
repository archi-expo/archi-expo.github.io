The making of a modern clinic

Health & Wellness Centre in Santa Lucija

Matthew Grech

In the initial phases, the concept of the project started from the idea of being permeable to its surroundings. The overall building envelope is not exactly small however when one enters the buildings there is a sense of openness that is felt throughout the bottom floor especially. This was to promote movement in between and through the buildings with everywhere being highly accessible. Another part which also was the basis of this project was the perseverance to design for the future not just sustainably but also technologically. When one looks at the medical system locally, we notice how most of the practises have been in use since the 50s, and considering 60 years or so have passed, one would think the system would have evolved with the generations, but that is not the case. We find ourselves going to a medical centre filled with regret and almost annoyed at the experience we are about to endure, waiting in line to get passed onto another room to wait until you get called to go into a dull and dark doctors office only to be redirected again to collect medical supplies from another part of the building. In this proposal there are more efficient ways of dealing with this method while also accommodating different individuals from different backgrounds of all ages. As mentioned before, a lot of the current generations are very aware of the information available at their finger tips, and many try to self diagnose themselves first before choosing to go to a doctor. Sometimes we even find ourselves knowing what medication we need to buy but need a doctor’s certificate for it. But certain places around the world have started to bypass this system by installing machines that one can find at a medical centre to ‘self diagnose’ themselves and access the required information more quickly. Of course this would not work with everything and for everyone but it would significantly cut down on people going to hospital emergency for no reason or lining up to see the doctor. If this process is not successful then an appointment can be booked while referring you to the appropriate doctor. An app can also be designed for everyone to download for helpful alerts, set reminders easily for appointments and a small interior map of where the doctor’s room would be on which floor. A panel within different parts of the building will also be available to get any information and updates required as to not solely rely on someone installing the app. The secondary building flanking the main building consists of a relaxation/meditation area with small pods housing a massage chair that can isolate itself from the outside world both in terms of sounds and space. These can be used by everyone, while also being connected to the app to see when one is available for booking.

From the interviews conducted in Santa Lucija, we also got to know that what residents really need is a place that has everything under one roof. This does not imply that they want a hospital but rather a place where one can go when common problems and questions might arise and to build a relationship with the people that work there to further integrate with the area. Another observation made of the locality is some middle aged / elderly people walking around the green areas, almost lingering, without a purpose other than to engage in light conversation with someone else. Within this redesign this was one of the strongest parts of the initial stages. The word ‘linger’ was used to analyse different areas within the project, giving the opportunity to walk around both while using the facilities in the building or not, it is designed to be completely public at all times of the day. This system of walkways and paths all lead to places in and around the area. Some take you to the other side, some will loop back on themselves and some take you to a little ‘piazza’ with a small water feature, with water sounds. This is all to help remove oneself from the busy lives we might be encountering at the time, or just purely a place to relax. As mentioned before it is open for all, making it useful even to those waiting for someone else at the clinic.
